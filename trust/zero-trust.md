# Zero-Trust (ZT)

Zero‑trust is the **ground state** of the trust pipeline.  
It assumes nothing, privileges nothing, and treats every component as potentially adversarial until proven otherwise.

Zero‑trust is not fear.  
It is **clarity**.

---

## Core Principles

- **Assume compromise** — hardware, firmware, software, networks  
- **Verify everything** — no implicit trust paths  
- **Authenticate continuously** — identity is not a one‑time event  
- **Minimise assumptions** — every assumption is an attack surface  
- **Isolate components** — boundaries create safety  

Zero‑trust is the epistemic equivalent of a clean slate.

---

## Zero‑Trust in Practice

- The drive is untrusted  
- The bootloader is untrusted  
- The OS is untrusted  
- The network is untrusted  
- Even your own prior conclusions are untrusted  

This is the correct starting point for the civilisation‑stack.

## Purpose of Zero‑Trust

Zero‑trust clears the ground.  
It removes illusions.  
It forces the system to justify itself through behaviour, not assumption.

It is the **beginning of all trustworthy architecture**.
