GLIRL Continuity Maps (v0.1)

Long‑Horizon Identity, Narrative, and Structural Continuity



1\. Purpose of Continuity Maps

Continuity Maps answer the civilisation’s most important structural question:



How does anything remain itself while changing across time?



Continuity Maps ensure that:



identity evolves without breaking



narratives progress without collapsing



capabilities grow without fragmenting



figments branch without losing coherence



redeclarations remain traceable



transitions remain invariant‑preserving



They are the deep‑time substrate of the Civilisation‑Stack.



2\. What a Continuity Map Is

A Continuity Map is a structured GLIRL object:



Code

CONTINUITY\_MAP(identity, figments, actions, arcs, invariants)

It encodes:



identity lineage



figment constellation evolution



action history



narrative arcs



invariant preservation



A Continuity Map is not a log.

It is a structural model of becoming.



3\. Continuity vs History

History is what happened.

Continuity is how what happened remains meaningful.



History is chronological.

Continuity is structural.



History records events.

Continuity records relationships between events.



Continuity Maps ensure:



no silent jumps



no identity resets



no narrative breaks



no unverified transitions



no invariant violations



Continuity is the civilisation’s spine.



4\. Components of a Continuity Map

A Continuity Map consists of five layers:



Identity Continuity



Figment Continuity



Action Continuity



Narrative Continuity



Invariant Continuity



Each layer is evaluated using GLIRL operators.



5\. Identity Continuity

Identity continuity ensures:



identity evolves



but never breaks



never resets



never becomes untraceable



Formal rule:



Code

CONTINUES(identity\_prev → identity\_next) = TRUE

Identity evolution is a path, not a jump.



6\. Figment Continuity

Figment continuity tracks:



how figments emerge



how they relate



how they collapse



how they evolve into actions



Rule:



Code

figment\_next must derive from figment\_prev

No figment may appear ex nihilo.



Figment continuity ensures the civilisation’s adjacent possible remains coherent.



7\. Action Continuity

Actions must:



derive from figments



preserve invariants



maintain narrative alignment



be witnessed



Rule:



Code

ACTION = COLLAPSE(FIGMENT)

If a figment cannot collapse cleanly → action is blocked.



8\. Narrative Continuity

Narrative continuity ensures:



arcs remain coherent



roles remain interpretable



symbolic meaning persists



mythic structure remains intact



Rule:



Code

ARC(a → b) must be valid

BREAKS\_ARC(a, b) = FALSE

Narrative continuity is the civilisation’s story logic.



9\. Invariant Continuity

Invariant continuity ensures:



no transition violates invariants



no redeclaration breaks structure



no figment collapse violates rules



no cross‑layer operation contradicts physics



Rule:



Code

VIOLATES(expr, invariant) = FALSE

Invariant continuity is the civilisation’s safety rail.



10\. Continuity Map Construction

A Continuity Map is constructed incrementally:



10.1 Event Occurs

An event may be:



action



redeclaration



figment collapse



arena transition



TRU issuance



10.2 Witness Network Observes

Witnesses evaluate:



invariants



continuity



narrative alignment



cross‑layer coherence



10.3 GLIRL Evaluates

GLIRL reduces the continuity expression:



Code

CONTINUITY(expr) → TRUE | FALSE

10.4 Map Updates

If TRUE → continuity map extends

If FALSE → event is blocked



Continuity Maps are self‑maintaining.

13\. Continuity Breaks

A continuity break occurs when:



identity jumps



narrative collapses



invariants are violated



figments appear without lineage



actions occur without figments



redeclarations skip structure



When a break is detected:



The event is blocked



The Witness Network records the violation



The identity vector is updated



The narrative arc is adjusted



The figment constellation is recalculated



Continuity breaks are never silent.
